The problem

Every online solar configurator returns a system recommendation and a payback estimate. The number is too optimistic in at least three predictable ways — shading (satellite tools miss the 4pm redwood), on-peak vs. annual energy (configurators divide annual kWh output by annual consumption as if your utility bill were uniform), and export-rate economics (post-NEM-3.0, energy exported to the grid is credited at ~$0.05/kWh, not the ~$0.38/kWh import rate). None of this is dishonest on purpose; the installer is paid per watt installed and the configurator is paid per lead generated. The optimism is structural.

What it is

A twenty-row spreadsheet that sizes a PV system from first principles — roof geometry, NREL/PVGIS irradiance, actual shading derate from a roof walk, tariff-schedule-weighted savings, and the self-consumption sweet spot that NEM-3.0 created. Output: a recommended kWp, a net cost after incentives (DSIRE), and a simple payback that holds up under scrutiny. The same kernel, extended with demand-charge and PPA rows, is the pro forma for a 50–500 kWp commercial-rooftop project. The arithmetic runs in the demo beside this sheet — the worked San Jose scenario, plus a live mode: your coordinates pull real NREL PVWatts production and your utility's actual filed tariffs (OpenEI URDB), and the twenty rows come back as a pro-forma CSV.

The wedge — vs. every online configurator

The configurator is fast and free, and the hardware math is fine. What it structurally can't show you is a reason to buy less.

The commercial pro forma offer

  1. Scoping call. Confirm roof area, tariff structure, ownership model (PPA/lease/direct), lender requirements.
  2. Site inputs. Pull NREL/PVGIS irradiance; walk or review shading report; get three competitive EPC quotes.
  3. Pro forma build. Twenty-row kernel + demand-charge, depreciation (MACRS), PPA/IRR, and simple payback rows. Sensitivity table on export rate and system size.
  4. Deliverable. Spreadsheet + one-page narrative — a documented model, yours to own.

Handed over at completion. Not a black-box quote — a model your lender's engineer can audit row by row.
