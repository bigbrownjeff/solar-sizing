Provenance — every call, source, and assumption

This page makes no API call about your roof. The yield numbers come from a surface of 120 PVGIS PVcalc calls (every 15° of azimuth × five tilts) pre-fetched for , embedded below, and interpolated as you drag. The browser computes the rest.

Assumptions (all editable above or by dragging):

  • Specific yield interpolated from PVGIS at 14% system loss, then × your roof-walk derate.
  • kWp = panel area × density (default 200 W/m², ~20% modules). Drag a corner to change area.
  • Import rate defaults to the LIPA tariff-weighted rate from a real proforma.py run; set yours.
  • Export credit defaults to ~$0.19, the PSEG-LI Rate 194 off-peak rate where most solar generation banks under NY time-of-day net metering. (This is NOT California NEM-3.0's ~$0.05 — the export rule is jurisdiction-specific; set yours.)
  • Federal ITC defaults to 0 — Section 25D expired 12/31/2025 for owned 2026 installs (third-party leases keep 48E). NY's 25%/$5k state credit is pre-filled. Self-consumption, $/Wp, 5% discount, 25 yr, 0.5%/yr degradation match proforma.py.
  • US note: PVGIS here uses PVGIS-NSRDB, the same NSRDB NREL PVWatts uses — so they are not independent; NASA POWER is the independent check.

What this is, and isn't. Production is a typical PVGIS year (2005–2015 average), not your weather; a lender-grade pro forma carries P50/P90 context. The 0.85 derate is a deliberate, conservative shading knob for a typical roof; a well-sited, unshaded array is closer to 0.95–1.0 — validated against real systems (see the repo's validation set).
