The reason to buy less — past the self-consumption sweet spot, the next kWp is mostly exported. How the arithmetic works
Napkin-checkable, per the note: every constant is FIG.1's.
  • Yield: specific yield = 1,586 kWh/kWp/yr pre-shading (FIG.1's basis: ~1,348 ÷ 0.85) × your derate.
  • Size: the honest column holds FIG.1's sweet-spot energy target (9,706 kWh/yr ≈ 69% annual offset) — a dirtier roof needs more panels to hit the same energy, so size and cost rise as derate falls. The configurator column sizes to 100% annual offset at a 0.97 derate; it never moves, because the configurator doesn't ask.
  • Savings: 55% of generation is self-consumed (FIG.1's scenario input) and displaces imports at $0.313/kWh — the all-hours tariff-weighted rate FIG.1's savings row implies, below the $0.38 on-peak headline. The other 45% earns your export-credit slider.
  • Payback: net cost ÷ annual savings. No federal ITC (Section 25D expired 12/31/2025); no discounting, no escalation — simple on purpose.

Type your roof's coordinates (long-press it in any maps app) and the size on your quote. One click fetches real production from NREL PVWatts v8 (NSRDB typical-year weather, hourly) and real filed tariffs from the OpenEI Utility Rate Database, then runs the note's twenty-row pro forma on them — savings weighted by when the panels actually produce, not by annual % offset.
